Abstract

This paper presents a groundbreaking approach to enhancing qubit stability in quantum computing systems. By leveraging a novel combination of superconducting circuits and topological insulators, we demonstrate a significant increase in coherence times, paving the way for more robust quantum computations. Our findings suggest a potential pathway towards scalable quantum computers with reduced error rates and improved overall performance.

Experiment Description

Our experiment involved the fabrication of a novel qubit design incorporating topological insulator materials. We conducted a series of tests to measure coherence times and gate fidelities under various conditions.

Results
